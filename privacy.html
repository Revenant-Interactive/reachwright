Privacy, in plain English

Last updated: July 15, 2026. This page is short because we collect almost nothing.

The demo

The qualification demo on our homepage currently runs entirely in your browser. Your answers are never transmitted, logged, or stored anywhere — close the tab and they're gone. The demo never asks for your name, email, phone number, or any personal information.

When a live-AI mode launches, this page will change first: it will name every provider a demo message passes through and what each one retains, before the feature goes live. We will not claim "nothing is stored" unless it's verified true.

This website

  • We set no analytics or tracking cookies of our own.
  • We run no third-party analytics or ad trackers.
  • The site is hosted on GitHub Pages; GitHub receives standard web-server request data (IP address, user agent) as any host does — see GitHub's privacy statement.

Booking a call

If you email us, we get your email — that's the point — and we use it only to talk to you about your inquiry. When online scheduling launches (Calendly), the handoff will be disclosed right at the booking step, and Calendly's own privacy policy governs what you enter there. We never sell or share your contact information.

Opting out

Say "stop" — in any channel, in any words — and we stop. That's the whole policy.
