# Reachwright revenue operating plan

## Current stage

Reachwright is an **owner-operated proof and revenue-validation system**. It is
not an autonomous prospecting agent, a commercial data provider, a bulk sender,
or a multi-client portal. Its current job is narrower and more useful:

1. identify a current, supportable reason a company may need copy capacity;
2. connect that reason to one sellable Reemergence service;
3. resolve a permitted route to an appropriate person;
4. prepare evidence-bound options for Michael to review and send manually;
5. record what actually happens through call, agreement, payment, and retained
   revenue.

## The $10k model in the product

The local Revenue screen defaults to:

| input | planning value |
|---|---:|
| target recurring revenue | $10,000/month |
| average retained client | $1,500/month |
| retained clients required from zero | 7 |
| held-call close assumption | 25% |
| booking show assumption | 70% |
| positive-reply to booking assumption | 50% |
| manual outreach to positive-reply assumption | 10% |

Those rates are planning assumptions, not Reachwright performance claims or
industry benchmarks. Change them as soon as observed data exists. At the
defaults, the reverse plan is 28 held calls, 40 bookings, 80 positive replies,
and 800 manual outreaches. That arithmetic is a diagnostic: if the activity is
unworkable, improve the offer, source quality, message, or economics instead of
pretending the target will happen automatically.

## What the outside scale plan got right

- Sell a software-enabled service before pretending Reachwright is finished
  SaaS.
- Keep research, qualification, copy diagnosis, message creation, pipeline,
  and outcomes in one owner workflow.
- Make the reason to contact and the evidence-backed copy opportunity the
  differentiator.
- Use provider adapters and client-funded infrastructure rather than building
  a proprietary contact database or sender network.
- Keep compliance, suppression, consent boundaries, and human approval as
  product gates.

## What Reachwright already has

- editable copywriting service and signal models;
- broad market lanes rather than a roofing-only workflow;
- bounded discovery with provider attribution and credit ceilings;
- official-site research, evidence records, freshness, and source URLs;
- hard qualification gates where strong ICP fit cannot compensate for no copy
  opportunity or no buying capacity;
- decision-maker and contact-route verification states;
- service matching and three human-review message options;
- manual approval, packet hashing, suppression, attempt/outcome recording;
- booked versus held calls, offer snapshots, agreement state, and
  operator-confirmed payment;
- campaign, client, yield, cost, feedback, and revenue reporting.

## Gaps that matter now

| gap | decision |
|---|---|
| false hiring signals from navigation or service copy | fixed: exact role-shaped matching is required |
| weak page defects treated as sales demand | fixed: missing H1 no longer qualifies outreach |
| generic detector-language messages | fixed for hiring: role/capacity/paid-trial messages |
| no visible path from activity to $10k MRR | fixed: Revenue command center and reverse plan |
| source pages that block automated retrieval | operator must verify; blocked evidence cannot be silently upgraded |
| no production scheduling link | fixed: public Cal.com fit-call event is configured and tested; cancellation/reschedule notification QA remains a launch gate |
| no direct contract/job opportunity lane | next discovery expansion: permitted ATS-board sources and an application workflow |
| no churn, pause, or contract-end model | add after the first recurring client is recorded |
| no client login or hard tenant isolation | defer until retained client work proves a need |
| no automated sequences or unified inbox | defer until sender identity, compliance, volume, and channel economics are proven |

## Thirty-day owner cadence

### Week 1: prove precision

- Work only candidates with a cited current copy opportunity, capacity signal,
  and verified contact route.
- Verify every source before selecting a message.
- Send manually and record every send, reply, and rejection reason.
- Keep the offer narrow: a defined paid project or $1,500/month retained
  capacity, not an undefined menu of services.

### Week 2: prove a conversation pattern

- Review cohorts by signal, market, provider, service, and message strategy.
- Stop any lane producing unverifiable demand or repeated no-fit outcomes.
- Preserve the wording and service pairing that produces positive replies.
- Turn positive replies into a scheduled fit conversation with a tested booking
  link or explicitly agreed time.

### Week 3: prove delivery and payment

- Recommend the smallest responsible offer from the sales-call console.
- Use written scope/agreement, signature, and cleared payment before work.
- Deliver one bounded piece of copy with an agreed acceptance criterion.
- Record paid state only after operator confirmation.

### Week 4: decide what earns automation

- Replace planning assumptions with observed funnel rates.
- Calculate time per researched candidate, positive reply, held call, and sale.
- Automate only the repeated bottleneck with enough evidence to justify it.
- Add a client workspace, sequence engine, or inbox only if live paid work now
  requires that capability.

## Build and stop rules

Build next when it shortens a measured bottleneck or protects truth, safety, or
delivery quality. Stop building when the next result depends on Michael sending
the reviewed message, holding the call, signing the agreement, or delivering
the work. Reachwright can prioritize and prepare those actions; it cannot
honestly substitute activity for customer demand.

No automatic sending is authorized by this plan. No production deployment,
provider purchase, booking-account creation, or external account change occurs
without the owner's explicit decision.
